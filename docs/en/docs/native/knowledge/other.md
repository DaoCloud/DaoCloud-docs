# Others

This page lists some other matters and articles in the cloud native community.

- [How Cloud Native Changes Telecom Standards: Top-Down vs Bottom-Up](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/dH_3CAd0PS3EPYKpJA2yNQ)

    This article introduces cloud native's bottom-up development principles, best practices, and logic of de facto standards, as well as top-down, committee-driven standards like those in the telecom industry.
    What happens when top-down and bottom-up standards meet? What happens when protocol managers meet cloud native standards?

- [Taking Over K8s Deployment and Operations, Is the Infrastructure Team Ready?](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/7Y5GfShZhFwBQkQfRUPnoA)

    Kubernetes is very suitable as an Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS), providing on public clouds or as a new generation of infrastructure on enterprise private clouds, providing reliable infrastructure for modern applications.
    Therefore, the construction and operation of Kubernetes will be increasingly handed to infrastructure teams.
    This article explores why infrastructure teams are suitable for managing Kubernetes operations, and whether such a transformation is feasible.

- [2022 Cloud Native Technology Review and Outlook](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/yRMTS5z15-PERwlameMOIw)

    This article reviews the development of cloud native in 2022 from two aspects: underlying basic technology and scenario-based application technology: co-location brings efficiency improvement;
    Serverless completes standardization based on containers; Service Mesh tries new methods, implementation methods are still being explored; under the theme of cost reduction and efficiency increase, FinOps concept develops rapidly, etc.
